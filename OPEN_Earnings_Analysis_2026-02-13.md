## 6. iBuying Business Model Risks

### Core Business Model
Opendoor operates as an **iBuyer** (instant buyer):
1. Makes instant cash offers on homes
2. Purchases properties directly
3. Makes repairs/renovations
4. Re-lists for sale

### Critical Risks

#### A. Inventory Risk
- **Holds inventory of homes** during ownership period
- **Housing price declines** directly hit balance sheet
- **Interest rate increases** increase carrying costs
- **Illiquid inventory** cannot be quickly offloaded

#### B. Margin Compression
- **Spread between buy/sell prices** is the profit
- **Competition from Zillow, Redfin, Offerpad** compresses margins
- **Transaction volume decline** in housing downturn
- **High operational costs** for repairs/rehab

#### C. Leverage Risk
- **Total Debt/Equity: 220%** ‚ö†Ô∏è (VERY HIGH)
- **Heavily leveraged** to real estate prices
- **Debt servicing costs** rising with rates
- **Limited financial flexibility** in downturn

#### D. Path to Profitability
- **Target: Late 2026**
- Currently burning cash ($317M loss recently)
- Requires housing market cooperation
- May need additional capital raises

### Housing Downturn Survival Question
**Can they survive a housing downturn?** 
- Uncertain. Their 2020 near-death experience (COVID) showed vulnerability
- Layoffs of 35% of workforce in 2020
- Suspend home buying during COVID
- Business model requires stable or rising home prices
- A severe downturn could force asset fire sales
