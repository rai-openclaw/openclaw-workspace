## KEY MACRO THEMES & TAKEAWAYS

### 1. Fed Pivot Completed
- Started 2025 on hold; ended with three consecutive cuts
- Total easing: 75bp (4.50% → 3.75% effective rate)
- Shift from inflation-fighting to employment-supporting
- QT (balance sheet reduction) ended December 1, 2025

### 2. Stagflation Fears
- May FOMC statement explicitly noted "risks of higher unemployment AND higher inflation have risen"
- First time since early 1980s Fed faced simultaneous growth and inflation concerns
- Tariff policy created supply-side inflation pressure while growth slowed

### 3. Labor Market Deterioration
- Unemployment rose from 4.1% to 4.3%
- Job openings fell from 8.1M to 6.5M (-20%)
- **CRITICAL:** 2025 jobs data massively overstated - final revision showed only 181K jobs created vs 584K initially reported
- Average monthly payroll gain: Only 15K (revised) vs 49K (initial)

### 4. Inflation Persistence
- Core CPI started year at 3.3%, ended at 3.0% - minimal progress
- Services inflation remained sticky (shelter, healthcare)
- Tariff impacts emerged in Q4, pressuring goods prices
- Never reached Fed's 2% target during the year

### 5. Market Volatility
- S&P 500 experienced multiple 5%+ drawdowns on tariff/uncertainty fears
- 10Y Treasury yield ranged 3.9% - 4.7%
- Fed policy uncertainty drove equity volatility
- Financial conditions tightened despite rate cuts
